What Are Peptides?

Peptides are short chains of amino acids that play a vital role in many biological functions. In medicine and research, peptides are used for purposes ranging from weight management to muscle recovery, anti-aging, and more. Common therapeutic peptides include GLP-1 receptor agonists such as semaglutide and tirzepatide.

Understanding GLP-1 Peptides

GLP-1 peptides help regulate blood sugar, appetite, and digestion. These compounds are frequently used in the treatment of Type 2 diabetes and weight loss protocols. Examples include:

  • Semaglutide (e.g., Ozempic, Wegovy)
  • Tirzepatide (e.g., Mounjaro, Zepbound)

When reconstituting these peptides, dosage precision is key, which is where PeptiCalc comes in.

How Reconstitution Works

Most peptides come in a lyophilized (freeze-dried) form and need to be mixed with bacteriostatic water. This process is known as reconstitution. Once reconstituted, users can measure out precise doses using insulin syringes or mL-based measurements. 1 mL = 100 insulin units.

Tips for Accurate Dosing

  • Store reconstituted peptides in the fridge as directed by the manufacturer.
